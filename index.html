Earthquakes, one of the most catastrophic natural disasters known to humankind, are happending frequently around the world. Also, earthquakes are geological events that can strike with little warning, causing widespread destruction and loss of life. Understanding the trends and potential locations of earthquakes and their effects on the planet is crucial for us, scientists, and policymakers to develop effective strategies for mitigating their impact. While small earthquakes happening every day around the world, we focus on large scale earthquakes that may cause disasters to human and our environments. In the following questions and visulizations about the 782 huge earthquakes worldwise, which starting from Januarty 1st 2001 to January 1st 2023,

Question 3: Is there any relation between the significance of earthquakes and the maximum estimated instrumental intensity can get?

Try the Brushing for Selecting Points

Information about This Visualization

This demonstrating that when the instrument detected large intensity on a earthquake, people and government should take more actions to prevent further following damages of the earthquakes.

Question 4: What is the distribution of average earthquakes over years?

Channels The X position channel represents the year.

From the bar plot, it can be found out that the year 2009, 2012, and 2021 have larger scale earthquakes on average than all other years. While the earthquakes are such an unpredicting activities causing by collisions under the ground, we may notice that following the years which have relatively lower average magnitude of earthquake, there will be larger average magnitude of earthquakes in the next few years. This gives us hint that we need to cautious about the year which the magnitude of earthquakes that happended in the previous years are not super high.

Question 6: Is there any difference of average magnitudes between tsunami and non-tsunami earthquakes?

Information about This Visualization

Color of Scheme The color for box of 25% - 75% part is blue, and red for 50% line
Marks The boxes that represent the 25th and 75th quartilesof magnitude values.
Channels The X position channel represents whether the Tsunami is triggered or not.
The Y position channel represents the magnitude of large earthquakes.

From the box plot, we found out that 50% of earthquakes for both Tsunami tiggered or not have magnitude of 6 and 75% of earthquakes are under magnitude of 7. It is not clear that there is a strong relationship between the happening of tsunima and the magnitude of the earthquakes. This also demonstrated that the cause of Tsunami may not only depend on the magnitude of earthquake, although we sometimes think the larger earthquakes are more like to trigger tsunami. The predictions of tsunami also needs the geographic locations for the earthquakes for people and government.

Conclusion:

By the explorations on huge earthquakes data, we can find the earthquake bands on the map, and find out that more earthquakes were happening along the costal areas. By tracking earthquake frequency, magnitude, and location, scientists can better understand the patterns of seismic activity and develop models for predicting future earthquakes.

However, it's important to note that the nature of earthquakes is still unpredictable and there will always be some level of uncertainty in earthquake forecasting. Despite advances in technology and seismic monitoring, it's impossible to predict exactly when and where an earthquake will occur or how severe it will be.

Therefore, it's important for individuals and communities to be prepared for earthquakes by taking steps such as securing buildings and having emergency plans in place.
